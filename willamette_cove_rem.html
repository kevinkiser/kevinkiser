This project presents a data visualization of contaminants at the Portland Willamette Cove Superfund site, using R to highlight contamination levels relative to human and ecological health thresholds. The goal is to provide decision-makers with a clear, interactive tool to identify high-priority remediation areas while minimizing cleanup costs. The visualization, built within a Shiny App, allows users to explore contamination distribution across the site at varying depths and thresholds. The challenge lies in balancing the need for effective remediation with the potential costs of over-cleaning, making this a complex decision for stakeholders. By effectively targeting the most contaminated areas, the project aims to support the transformation of Willamette Cove into a safe, accessible community nature park.
